# Tio Javi's Heritage Cantina Design

Date: 2026-05-27

## Goal

Refine the existing Tio Javi's portfolio project without replacing its strongest qualities. The site should keep its dark, photo-led, polished restaurant mood while feeling more connected to the Tio Javi's logo and less generic in typography.

The approved direction is **Heritage Cantina**: upscale local favorite. It should feel established, warm, energetic, and a little custom, without becoming a loud themed cantina or a quiet fine-dining template.

## Visual Direction

- Keep the site dark and image-led.
- Do not use patterned backgrounds, repeated decorative linework, or anything that reads like wrapping paper.
- Use deep navy as a structural surface color, especially in nav, menu tabs, section bands, and footer areas.
- Use orange/gold for actions, prices, selected states, and small moments of warmth.
- Use teal sparingly as a logo echo in borders, rules, or tiny details.
- Let food and interior photos provide the energy instead of adding busy decoration.

## Typography

The current typography is attractive but slightly too familiar. The redesign should introduce more character while keeping menu readability.

- Replace the basic body/nav sans with a warmer, less default-feeling readable font.
- Keep an elegant serif for emotional hero and section moments, but tune usage so it feels intentional rather than generic.
- Use a punchier display/accent face for buttons, prices, tabs, or labels where a sign-painter/menu-board feeling helps.
- Avoid cramped or overly decorative body copy.

## Menu Pages

The menu pages are the main improvement target. They should stop feeling like long dark lists and start feeling like branded menu experiences.

- Preserve the sticky Lunch / Dinner / Bar tabs.
- Make the menu hero and tab areas feel more tied to the logo with navy surfaces and cleaner accent states.
- Improve section rhythm with stronger section headers, spacing, and grouped visual hierarchy.
- Use photos as intentional anchors between major menu groups, not blurry wallpaper.
- Keep the menu easy to scan: item name, description, variants, and prices must stay clear.

## Home, Nav, And Footer

The home page already has a strong mood. Changes should be restrained and supportive.

- Keep the full-screen photo hero.
- Introduce navy subtly through overlays and surface treatments.
- Keep orange/gold calls to action, but balance them with the deeper navy system.
- Make the nav feel more branded and less plain black glass.
- Bring footer styling into the same navy/gold/cream language.

## Constraints

- Do not add heavy new dependencies unless there is a clear need.
- Keep changes scoped to visual polish and menu presentation.
- Do not rewrite page content or routing.
- Avoid broad refactors unless needed to share repeated menu styling cleanly.
- Preserve mobile usability and readable menu text.

## Verification

- Run lint/build checks after implementation.
- Inspect the live site in the in-app browser across home, dinner menu, lunch menu, bar, and visit pages.
- Check mobile layout for nav, menu tabs, menu rows, photo breaks, and footer.
- Confirm the final design does not use the rejected patterned background direction.
